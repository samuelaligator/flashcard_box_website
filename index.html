How the Leitner System Works

The Leitner system is a simple yet effective method to enhance your memorization process. Flashcards are organized into compartments, each representing a review interval:

  • Initial Compartment: New cards or those that need immediate review.
  • Intermediate Compartments: Cards you know moderately, moving gradually to less frequent reviews.
  • Advanced Compartment: Well-mastered cards that require minimal revision.

Diagram illustrating the Leitner system compartments.

Benefits of Spaced Repetition

Spaced repetition is a scientifically proven technique to improve long-term memory retention. By increasing the intervals between reviews, you reinforce learning and reduce the time spent on material you already know well.

Using our flashcard box with the Leitner method, you ensure that your study sessions are both efficient and effective, allowing you to focus on the areas that need the most attention.

Printing & Assembly Guide

Printing Recommendations

For best results, we recommend printing with PLA filament using a nozzle temperature of approximately 200°C and a heated bed at 60°C. Two STL files are provided to suit your needs:

  • 0.2 mm Clearance (Recommended): Provides an easy-to-print and assemble model with a reliable fit.
  • 0.1 mm Clearance: Offers a tighter fit for those who prefer a more precise assembly.

Creating Your Own A8 Flashcards

To create A8 sized flashcards, simply cut an A4 sheet in half four times in a row. This method yields eight perfectly sized A8 cards (52 × 74 mm) ideal for your study needs.
